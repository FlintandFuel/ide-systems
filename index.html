The Problem

Cooperative banking in South Africa is underserved by every system that exists.

Thirty registered cooperative financial institutions hold over R656 million in member deposits. They operate under the Cooperative Banks Act and answer to the Prudential Authority. The stakes are real.

Yet most software available to them was built for commercial banks, global credit unions, or informal savings groups. Not quite any of them. Generic platforms require expensive adaptation. Foreign solutions don't know South African regulation. Local developers often lack the banking depth to build something trustworthy.

The institutions doing the most important work in South Africa, community upliftment, member empowerment, financial inclusion in underserved areas, are making do with tools that were never designed for them.

Compliance and Security

Compliance isn't a feature.
It's the foundation.

Registered CFIs operate in a heavily supervised environment. Paxiss was designed with that reality at its core, not adapted to it after the fact.

SARB Prudential Authority

Paxiss is built to the reporting and operational standards required by the Prudential Authority for registered CFIs. Quarterly returns, compliance submissions, and capital adequacy tracking are all supported.

The system generates reports in the formats the PA expects, reducing the manual reformatting that creates errors.

FICA and POPIA

Customer due diligence, identity verification, and KYC record management are integrated into member onboarding and ongoing transaction workflows. Not managed separately.

Data handling within Paxiss is designed to meet POPIA requirements. Member data stays where it belongs.

CODI Reporting

Co-operative Banks Development Agency reporting requirements are automated within the general ledger module. Month-end and year-end reporting becomes a process, not a crisis.

Paxiss generates CODI-compliant financial statements directly from live data.

Audit Trails and Forensics

Every transaction, user action, and system event is logged immutably. If something ever requires investigation, the record is there. This protects your institution and your members.

Role-based access control ensures no user has access beyond what their mandate specifies.
